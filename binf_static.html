What is Bioinformatics?

Bioinformatics is the study of the information underlying biology. When people think about research in medicine or biology, they usually picture someone in a lab coat running around, transferring DNA between test tubes. But while the physical DNA is important, much more interesting is the information it represents -- the genome. Thus, we must understand biology at its most fundamental level not as a physical science dealing with cells and molecules, but as an information science utilizing gene sequences and other data. Thus, bioinformatics is the exploration of the biological information underlying all life using computer science and statistics.

Why is Bioinformatics cool?

Bioinformatics is cool because it underpins our capacity to understand biological systems. Rapid advances in our ability to capture the information composing biological systems such as the cell have caused an explosion of data, trouncing traditional techniques of understanding this data and achieving biological insight. Only by drawing on statistics and computer science can we hope to marshal sufficient analytical power to make sense of this data. Thus, the bioinformatician stands at the crossroads of biology, computer science, and statistics, combining cutting-edge techniques from each to understand biological systems and improve health.
