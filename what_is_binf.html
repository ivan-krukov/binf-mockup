What is Bioinformatics?

It is rather hard to concisely explain what bioinformatics is. It is a very broad field, and different people may be using the term to describe rather different things. However, here is our best try:

"Bioinformatics is the science of organizing and processing information about life"

Over the last 10 years, biology has moved from a data-conservative endeavour to an increasingly data-intensive pursuit. The amount of information available to biologists has long crossed the point where it is possible to evaluate the information manually. Now, the only viable alternative is to study biology with a heavy use of computation. Based on this, here is another definition of bioinformatics:

"Bioinformatics is the new kind of biology, which studies life with the assistance of computation and statistics"

Here is a more concrete example:

Every living thing (and even some of the non-living) have a genome. A genome is like a biological blueprint that describes what an organism is or could be. This blueprint is composed of DNA, which has an alphabet of four letters: A, C, T, and G. Your genome has 3 billion of these letters that describe everything from your eye colour to the risk of diabetes. To get a better understanding of the amount of information, let us compare the human genome to a book.

War and Peace, a literary classic by Leo Tolstoy, is a rather thick read. It has around 3 million letters, which is around 1200 pages. This means that it will take us 1000 copies of War and Peace to collect all of the human genome information. So if you stack all these copies on top of each other, they will be a little taller than the MacKimmie Library tower in UofC. Now you can see why it is hard to analyze biological information by hand.

So, you eye colour will be equivalent to a single chapter in your genome book. A genetic disease can be compared to a typo in your genome. Since DNA is composed of only 4 letters, it is rather hard to just “read” it, so we need powerful computers and fancy mathematics to tease out its meaning.

Bioinformatics is a new field, but it already has many sub-disciplines. Computer science is essential to be able to organize and process information. Statistics and mathematics is indispensable to find and classify patterns. Biology is crucial to understand and apply the new knowledge. With this, we arrive to the third definition of bioinformatics:

"Bioinformatics is the interdisciplinary connection between biology, computer science and mathematics"
